Conditionals

Conditional sentences consider imagined or uncertain situations and the possible results of these situations. The most common types of conditional sentences involve the word if.

A conditional sentence has two parts. The conditional clause and the main clause.

Example:

If a lot of people come, we will have to get extra chairs.

The conditional clause is: If a lot of people come

The main clause is: we will have to get extra chairs


The order of the two clauses does not matter. The conditional clause can come before or after the main clause.

Example:

Conditional clause first: If a lot of people come, we will have to get extra chairs.

Conditional clause second: We will have to get extra chairs if a lot of people come.


IMPORTANT

If the conditional clause comes first, we have to add a comma at the end of the clause.
