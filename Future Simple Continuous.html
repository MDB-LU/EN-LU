Future Simple Continuous

We use the future simple continuous to refer to temporary actions and events that will be in progress at a particular time in the future.

To make the future simple continuous, we have to use the word will, the auxiliary verb be and the ing form of the main verb.

Examples:

This time next week, I will be taking photographs with my new camera.

Next week they will be flying to Australia from Saudi Arabia.


Negative

If we want to make a negative sentence in the future simple continuous, we have to add the word not after will and before be.

We can also use the abbreviation won't which stands for will not.

Examples:

Tomorrow, I will not be working from 8 to 10.

In two years, I won't be playing for the national team anymore.


Questions

We can also make questions in the future simple continuous. We just have to put will before the subject.

Examples:

Will they be working tomorrow?

Where will they be staying during their holidays?
