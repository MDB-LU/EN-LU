Present Simple - Third Person -S

The form of the verb in positive sentences and the auxiliary verb in negative sentences and questions in the Present Simple is the same in every person except for the third person singular. In the third person singular, we have to add the letter s to the end of the verb.

Examples:

First person singular: I like chocolate.

Second person singular: You like chocolate.

Third person singular: He likes chocolate. / She likes chocolate. / It likes chocolate.

First person plural: We like chocolate.

Second person plural: You like chocolate.

Third person plural: They like chocolate.


Present Simple - Negative sentences in the third person singular

If we want to make a negative sentence in the Present Simple which is in the third person singular, we have to use an auxiliary verb. Most of the time, this auxiliary verb is doesn't.

Doesn't stands for does not.

Examples:

He doesn't work in a bank.

She doesn't play football.

It doesn't work.


Present Simple - Questions in the third person singular

If we want to ask a question in the Present Simple which is in the third person, we have to use an auxiliary verb. Most of the time, this auxiliary verb is does.

Examples:

Does he work in a bank?

Does she play football?

Does it work?


IMPORTANT

When we use the auxiliary verb doesn't in negative sentences or does in questions, we have to use the base form of the main verb.

Examples:

He doesn't work in a bank. NOT: He doesn't works in a bank.

Does he work in a bank? NOT: Does he works in a bank?

She doesn't play football. NOT: She doesn't plays football.

Does she play football? NOT: Does she plays football?
