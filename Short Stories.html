Short Stories

This lesson is all about short stories. A short story is a brief fictional narrative that is shorter than a novel and that usually deals with only a few characters.

Read the descriptions of the five short stories and choose one of them. If you have selected a short story, download it, read it and take notes if necessary.

1. Civil Peace by Chinua Achebe

Civil Peace is a short story about the effects of the Nigerian Civil War on the people and the "civil peace" that followed.

Click here to read or listen to Civil Peace

2. Confido by Kurt Vonnegut

In Confido a laboratory assistant’s magical invention promises to put his family on easy street at last. But is a machine that gives voice to our innermost thoughts and unspoken grievances really the key to happiness or a direct line to despair?

Click here to read or listen to Confido

3. Désirée's Baby by Kate Chopin

Désirée's Baby is a short story is about racism and the mixing of races in Louisiana before the American Civil War.

Click here to read or listen to Désirée's Baby

4. Last Night of the World by Ray Bradbury

Last Night of the World is about how a couple spends their last night on earth.

Click here to read or listen to Last Night of the World

5. The Last Leaf by O. Henry

The Last Leaf is about Johnsy, who is very sick. She sees the falling leaves of the tree outside her window and thinks that she will die when all the leaves have fallen. But one leaf never falls.

Click here to read or listen to The Last Leaf



Follow-up Tasks:

Choose one of the three task to further work on the short story:


Task 1: Summary

Write a summary in English about the short story you have read. If you want to, you can send your summary via e-mail to Mr. Du Buisson.


Task 2: Presentation

Prepare a presentation of about 5-15 minutes. Summarize the short story, describe the characters and share your personal opinion. If the presentation is ready, present it to your English teacher.


Task 3: Review

Write a review about the short story. Summarize what the story is about and describe what you have liked and disliked. At the end of the review, say why people should read this short story or why not.
