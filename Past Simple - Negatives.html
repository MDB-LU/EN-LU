Past Simple - Negatives

If we want to use the negative in the Past Simple, we have to use an auxiliary verb. Most of the time this auxiliary verb is didn't. Didn't stands for did not.

Examples:

I didn't work in a bank.

We didn't play football.

They didn't buy a house.


We use the auxiliary verb didn't in every person.

Examples:

I didn't work

You didn't work

He didn't work / She didn't work / It didn't work

We didn't work

You didn't work

They didn't work


IMPORTANT

When we use the auxiliary verb didn't, we have to use the base form (infinitive) of the main verb.

Examples:

I didn't work in a bank. NOT: I didn't worked in a bank.

She didn't catch the ball. NOT: She didn't caught the ball.


Quick Check

Select the correct sentence.

1.

I didn't like the way he looked at me. I didn't liked the way he looked at me.

2.

He didn't go to school yesterday. He doesn't goes to school yesterday.

3.

They opened the window today, but yesterday they didn't. They opened the window today, but yesterday they don't.

4.

She didn't went on holiday with her family. She didn't go on holiday with her family.
