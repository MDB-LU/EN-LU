Present Perfect

We use the Present Perfect to refer to events in the past which connect to the present. In order to form the Present Perfect, we need the auxiliary verb have and the Past Participle of the main verb.

Examples:

You have lived in Liverpool for most of your life.

I have eaten too much.

He has watched a lot of movies lately.


Present Perfect - Negative

If we want to make a negative sentences in the Present Perfect, we need to add the word not. This word comes between the auxiliary verb and the main verb.

Examples:

I have not called home for a while.

You haven't played a lot of games recently.

He hasn't gone on holiday with his family.


Present Perfect - Questions

If we want to ask a question in the Present Perfect, we have to put the auxiliary verb have in front of the subject.

Examples:

Have you seen Jenny?

Have they stayed in a hotel?

Has he been to the doctor's yet?


IMPORTANT

We use the Present Perfect to talk about experiences up to now. The time of that experience is not important.

Example: I have seen Barbie in cinemas. In this sentence, the main message is that I have seen the film. It doesn't matter when that was.

We can also use the Present Perfect to talk about things that are not finished yet.

Example: I have visited my grandmother two times this year. The year is not yet finished, so I can still visit my grandmother more times.
