Zero Conditional

You already know that there are four types of conditional sentences. This one is called the Zero Conditional.

As every conditional sentence, this one also consists of two parts: The conditional clause and the main clause.


We can make a zero conditional sentence with two present simple verbs. One in the conditional clause and one in the main clause:

If + present simple / present simple

The zero conditional is used when the result will always happen. It's a fact. The result of the conditional clause is always the main clause.

Examples:

If water reaches 100 degrees, it always boils.

You get burned if you touch fire.

People die if they don't eat.


Quick Check

Select the correct zero conditional sentence:


1.

If you mix hydrogen and oxygen, you get water. If you mixed hydrogen and oxygen, you got water.

2.

3.

If babies are hungry, the cry. If babies are hungry they cry.
