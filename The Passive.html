The Passive

In English, we have the active and the passive voice. On this page, we will focus on the passive.


The Passive - Uses

We use the passive for multiple reasons:

1: We want to focus on the person or the thing affected by the action.

Example: Jurassic Park was directed by Steven Spielberg. (The focus of this sentence is on the film Jurassic Park)

2: We don't know who or what did an action.

Example: My car has been stolen. (I don't know who did it)

3: When it is not important or obvious who did an acion.

Example: The criminal is being arrested. (Here, we know that the police has arrested the criminal)


The Passive - Form

We make the passive form with the verb "be" and the past participle.

Examples:

This product is produced in China.

The story was reported in yesterday's newspaper.

A discovery has been made.


The Passive in different tenses

We can use the passive in different tenses. The past participle always stays the same, no matter what the tense we are in. The verb "be" always adopts the tense.

Examples:

Present Simple: A cake is made by my father.

Past Simple: E.T. was directed by Steven Spielberg.

Present Perfect: My car has been stolen.

Present Continuous: The criminal is being arrested by the police.
