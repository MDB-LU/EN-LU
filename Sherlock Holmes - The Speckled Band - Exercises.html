On this page, you will find some exercises for one of the Sherlock Holmes short stories.

Exercise 2.1

Do this exercise after you have read Chapter 2. Here are some false sentences. Change them into true sentences.

5: Dr Roylott did not need the girls' money.

6: Something was wrong with Helen's bedroom wall.

7: Helen and Julia often used the bell-rope.

8: The air-vent was outside the two bedrooms.


If you want to know whether your answers were correct or not, go to the end of this page to find the solutions.

Exercise 2.2

9: Dr Roylott kills Helen.

11: Dr Roylott dies.

12: Holmes finds the killer.

13: The gipsies kill Dr Roylott.





Solutions to Exercise 1

Doctor Grimesby Roylott is a violent man and killed an Indian servant.

Helen's mother died in an accident eight years ago.

Helen's sister died two years ago.

Helen and her sister Julia locked the doors because they were afraid of the wild animals.

Solutions to Exercise 2.1

5: Dr. Roylott needs the money because he only has 750£ a year from his dead wife.

6: Nothing was wrong with Helen's bedroom wall, which is why it is strange that they are fixing her bedroom.

7: Helen and Julia never used the bell-rope because the didn't have any servants.

8: The air-vent was between the two bedrooms of Doctor Roylott and Julia.
