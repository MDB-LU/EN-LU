Much and Many

Much and many are two words that we use a lot in English, especially in questions and negatives. But do you know when to use which one? And if you do, do you know why?

For this lesson, it is very important that you know what countable and uncountable nouns are. If you don't know what they are or if you need to refresh your memory, check out the following link:

Countable and uncountable nouns


Much

We use much in connection with nouns that are uncountable.

Examples:

How much coffee is there?

There isn't much coffee.


Many

We use many in connection with nouns that are countable.

Examples:

How many eggs are there?

There aren't many eggs.


IMPORTANT

Much and many are mainly used in questions and in negative sentences. In positive sentences, we mostly use a lot of

Examples:

There are a lot of books on the shelf.

He has a lot of money.
