Day 1

50 percent of a conversation is about understanding the other person. So listening is an important part of speaking. And this is exactly where we will start.

Listen to at least one of the recordings and write down five words you didn't understand in your worksheet.

Audio 1 - Try something new for 30 days

Audio 2 - The danger of silence

Audio 3 - Got a meeting? Take a walk!


Day 2

Have you written down five words in Task 1? Great! But now let's find out what they mean.

Using an online dictionary can be a great tool to find translations to uknown words.

Task 2

Check the five words from Task 1 and write down their translations using one of the online dictionaries. Write them down in your worksheet.

Task 3

You have learnt five new words, but there are many more to learn. Click on the following link and you will be directed to a page on which you will find thousands of words to learn. Choose at least one list on your level and practice thses words.

Vocabulary


Day 3

Reading aloud is a great way to improve fluency and pronunciation.

In your worksheet, you will find a text about the author Ray Bradbury. Read this text aloud.

Repeat this a couple of times and you will soon realize your improvement.


Day 4

The text about Ray Bradbury is not the easiest one. I'm sure that there are a couple of words you did not understand. So that is a great excuse to repeat the tips from day 1 and 2.

Write down 5 unknown words from the text in your worksheet. Find their translation with an online dictionary and write them down as well.
