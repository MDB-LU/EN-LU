Verb Forms

Verb forms generally have three forms:

1: base form (infinitive)

2: Past Simple form

3: Past Participle


Regular Verbs

With the regular verbs, you only have to remember two forms because the Past Simple and the Past Participle are the same.

Examples:

play - played - played

watch - watched - watched

stay - stayed - stayed


Irregular Verbs

Irregular verb forms are irregular, because they don't have the ending -ed for the Past Simple, and the Past Participle is not always the same as the Past Simple form.

Examples:

begin - began - begun

sit - sat - sat

drink - drank - drunk

IMPORTANT

Knowing these three forms of all the regular and irregular verbs is very important. If you know them, you already know enough to form every tense in English.
