Past Continuous

We use the Past Continuous to show a continuous action that began in the past. It is the past tense version of the present continuous, which also describes ongoing actions.

In order to form the Past Continuous, we need the auxiliary verbs was or were and the ing form of the main verb.

Examples:

He was going to the supermarket.

You were wearing a white shirt.

I was working in my garden.


Past Continuous - Negative

If we want to make a negative sentences in the Past Continuous, we need to add the word not. This word comes between the auxiliary verb and the main verb.

Examples:

I was not watching television.

You were not listening.

He wasn't cooking.


Past Continuous - Questions

If we want to ask a question in the Past Continuous, we have to put the auxiliary verb was or were in front of the subject.

Examples:

Were you working in your garden?

Was he eating his food?

What was I drinking?


Past Continuous - Uses

You now know how we make the Past Contiuous in positive and negative questions, as well as in questions. But when do we use this tense?

1. An action in the past that gets interrupted:

Example: Martha was leaving the apartment when she saw Jim.

2. Ongoing actions at a specified time in the past:

Example: At midnight, everybody was having fun.

3. Emphasizing how long a past action took:

Example: The athlete was training all year for this race.
