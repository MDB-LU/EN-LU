Future Intentions

You have already looked at the will future and the going to future, or you already know how they work. But do you also know that we can use both forms to talk about intentions or plans in the future?

Examples:

I am going to visit my grandmother next week.

You don't want to cook? That's ok, I will do it.


Both sentences are in the future, but they use a different future form. On this page, you will learn when to use which form.


Will Future

We use the will future when it is a spontaneous decision.

Examples:

Who wants to be the goalkeeper? I will do it.

The phone is ringing. I will pick it up.

Someone's knocking. I'll open the door.


Going to Future

We can the going to future to talk about definite plans.

Examples:

I'm going to watch the match tomorrow.

They are going to leave the country soon.

She's going to buy this car.

A: "Jim is back from holiday." B: "Is he? I'm going to give him a call."

A: "What are you doing tonight?" B: "We're going to see a play at the theatre."

"You can tell me your secret. I'm not going to tell anyone."

"Congratulations! I hear you will have a baby."
