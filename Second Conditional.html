Second Conditional

You already know that there are four types of conditional sentences. This one is called the Second Conditional.

As every conditional sentence, this one also consists of two parts: The conditional clause and the main clause.


We can make a second conditional sentence with the past simple in the conditional clause and would + infinitive in the main clause:

If + past simple / would + infinitive

We can use were instead of was with I and he, she, it.

The second conditional has two uses:

First, we can use it to talk about things in the future that are probably not going to be true.

Example:

If I won the lottery, I would buy a big house.


Second, we can use it to talk about something in the present which is impossible, because it's not true.

Example:

If I had his number, I would call him.

Quick Check

Select the correct second conditional sentence:


1.

She would travel all over the world if she were rich.
She will travel all over the world if she was rich.

2.

If I was you I wouldn't go out with that man.
If I were you, I wouldn't go out with that man.

3.

If Julie like chocolate, I will give her some.
If Julie liked chocolate, I would give her some.
