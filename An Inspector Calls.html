An Inspector Calls

An Inspector Calls is a play written by J.B. Priestley. The play is set in 1912 in the home of the prosperous Birling family. The family celebrates the engagement of their daughter Sheila, until Inspector Goole arrives to investigate the suicide of a young working-class woman called Eva Smith.

The story is about finding out what happened to Eva Smith and how she is (or is not) connected to the Birling family. It also covers timeless themes such as social justice, responsibility, the class system and the interconnection between human actions.
