Countable and Uncountable Nouns

In English, we have nouns that can be counted (countable nouns) and nouns that cannot be counted (uncountable nouns).


Countable Nouns

A countable noun is a noun that can be counted. For example:

a car, three cars

my book, my two favourite books

a tomato, some tomatoes


IMPORTANT

Countable nouns can be singluar or plural.


Uncountable Nouns

An uncountable noun is a noun that cannot be counted. For example:

Ideas and experiences: advice, information, progress, news, fun

Materials and substances: water, rice, sand, milk

Names for groups or collections of things: furniture, equipment, rubbish

Others: homework, money, traffic, travel


IMPORTANT

These uncountable nouns are NOT used with a / an or in the plural. For example:

We're going to get new furniture. NOT: We're going to get a new furniture.

I need more information. NOT: I need more informations.


BE CAREFUL

Some nouns always have a plural form but they are uncountable because we cannot use numbers with them. For example:

Scissors, trousers, pants, shorts, glasses (for the eyes)
