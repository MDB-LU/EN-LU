Going to Future

We use the going to future to refer to various events that happen in the future.

To make the going to future, we have to use the auxiliary verb be, the form going to and the base form (infinitive) of the main verb.

Examples:

I am going to take a few exams at the end of the year.

She is going to start a new job in July.

We're going to save money for a vacation.


Negative

If we want to make a negative sentence in the going to future, we have to add the word not after the auxiliary verb be and before going to.

Examples:

I am not going to fly ever again!

She is not going to miss the bus again.

My children aren't going to eat junk food anymore.


Questions

We can also make questions in the going to future. We just have to put the auxiliary verb be before the subject.

Examples:

Are you going to attend the meeting tomorrow?

Is she going to travel abroad this summer?

Are they going to invite us to their wedding?


IMPORTANT

We use the going to future to talk about future plans. Usually, these plans are quite definitive. There is a big chance that this will happen.

Example:

I am going to Basel tomorrow.

We're going to eat in my favourite restaurant tonight.

They're going to see a play next month.
