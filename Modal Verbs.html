Modal Verbs

Modal verbs are verbs that show possibility, intent, ability or necessity. Here are the most used modal verbs: can, could, may, might, should, will, would, must.

Examples:

I can drive a tractor.

I'm tired, I should go home.

I might go to the concert, but I'm not sure yet.


Modal verbs have the same form for every person:

I can

You can

He can / She can / It can

We can

You can

They can


Modal verbs are a type of auxiliary verb, so they generally can't be used on their own. They are used together with the main verb in the base form (infinitive).

Examples:

I can swim.

He should leave.

They will win.


Negatives

If we want to make a negative sentence with a modal verb, we have to add the word not.

Examples:

They might not win this game.

I should not be here. OR I shouldn't be here.


Questions

We can also ask questions. In that case, we just have to put the modal verb before the subject.

Examples:

Can I come home?

Should they win this award?

Will they perform their best song?
