Past Perfect

We use the Past Perfect to refer to a past action that occurred before another past action. In order to form the Past Perfect, we need the auxiliary verb had and the Past Participle of the main verb.

Examples:

They had worked together for years before they became friends.

When I got home, I realized I had left my keys in the office.

Nobody noticed that the thief had run away.


Past Perfect - Negative

If we want to make a negative sentences in the Past Perfect, we need to add the word not. This word comes between the auxiliary verb and the main verb.

Examples:

I had not eaten for three days, when they finally found me.

She hadn't had time to explain her side of the story.

My father hadn't gone to the United States either.


Past Perfect - Questions

If we want to ask a question in the Past Perfect, we have to put the auxiliary verb had in front of the subject.

Examples:

Had she eaten there before?

Where had they been?

Had he ever spoken to a celebrity before?


IMPORTANT

We use the Past Perfect to describe a past action that occurred before another action in the past.

Example: Jimmy had eaten at that restaurant many times before it closed.
