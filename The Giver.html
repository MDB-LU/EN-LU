The Giver

THE GIVER is the award-winning classic of bravery and adventure that has inspired countless dystopian writers as the forerunner of this genre. It is the future. There is no war, no hunger, no pain. No one in the community wants for anything. Everything needed is provided. And at twelve years old, each member of the community has their profession carefully chosen for them by the Committee of Elders. Twelve-year old Jonas has never thought there was anything wrong with his world. But from the moment he is selected as the Receiver of Memory, Jonas discovers that their community is not as perfect as it seems. It is only with the help of the Giver, that Jonas can find what has been lost. And it is only through his personal courage that Jonas finds the strength to do what is right...

For this book, there is no reading plan and there are no specific exercises that you have to do while reading. However, you could do a couple of tasks that make you work with the text in more detail. More information about possible tasks can be found at the end of this page.

Possible Tasks

There are no set exercises to be done for The Giver. However, there are a couple of possible tasks for you to do. Choose one or more of these if you want to to. As always, you have the possibility to send your texts to Mr Du Buisson (marc.dubuisson@sibu.ch).

Task 1:

Write a short summary of about 250 words for each chapter. This will allow you to keep track of the story long after you have finished reading the book. Send these summaries to Mr Du Buisson if you would like to have them checked.

Task 2:

If you have read the whole book, why not write a summary about it. Try to use 600 words at most to summarise the 237 pages of The Giver. Send the summary to Mr Du Buisson if you would like to have them checked.

Task 3:

Choose at least one of the characters of the book and describe them. What do they look like? What is their character like? How do they develop from the beginning of the book to the end of it? Send your observations to Mr Du Buisson if you would like to have them checked.

Task 4:

If you have read the whole book, why don't you write a review. Tell the reader what the book is about, what you like and don't like about it, what could have been done better, and why you recommend this book to others, or why not. Send this review to Mr Du Buisson if you would like to have it checked.
