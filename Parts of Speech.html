Parts of Speech

Every word in English can be classified as a part of speech (Wortart). On this page, we will focus on seven parts of speech.

1. Nouns

A noun is a word for a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are often used with an article, but not always.

Examples:

I live in a house.

Life is unfair.


2. Pronouns

A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun. There are several types of pronouns:


Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns refer to specific persons or things.

Personal pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they; me, him, her, us, them

Example:

I like her very much.


Possessive Pronouns

Possessive pronouns indicate ownership.

Possessive pronouns: mine, yours, his, hers.

Examples:

Do you see this car? That's mine.

whose hat is that? It's hers.

Relative Pronouns

Relative pronouns introduce a subordinate clause (Nebensatz).

Relative pronouns: that, which, who, whom, whose, what.

Example:

The teacher who helped me the most was Mr Kent.


Demonstrative Pronouns

Demonstrative pronouns identify, point to, or refer to nouns.

Demonstrative pronouns: this, that, these, those.

Examples:

Those flowers are beautiful.

This building is very old.


3. Verbs

The verb in a sentence expresses action or being. A verb can also take different forms to express when something happens.

Examples:

He plays the piano.

They played the guitar.

I will play the cello tomorrow.


4. Adjectives

An adjective is a word used to modify or describe a noun or a pronoun. We can change adjectives by making them stronger with the comparative and the superlative.

Examples:

The tree is big.

She is smaller than her sister.

New York is more dangerous than London.

This drink is the cheapest.

My car is the most expensive car ever.


5. Adverbs

An An adverb describes or modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb, but never a noun. Adverbs usually answer the questions of when, where, how, why, under what conditions, or to what degree. They often end with -ly.

Examples:

He sings badly.

Beth is incredibly proud.

She usually goes to church on Saturday.

I'm still waiting outside.


6. Prepositions

Prepositions link a word to another part of the sentence and tell us what the relationship is, for example in space or time.

Prepositions: in, at, on, since, for, until, by, between, of, with, from.

Examples:

We went to school on Monday.

I saw him at the weekend after the game.


7. Conjunction

A conjunction joins words, phrases, or clauses, and indicates the relationship between the elements joined.

Conjunctions: and, but, or, while, because, when, if.

Example:

I like cats and dogs, but I don't like horses.
