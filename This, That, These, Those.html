This, That, These, Those

The four words are used a lot in English. On this page, you will learn when to use which one.


This and That

We use this and that if we want to point out one thing (singular).

Examples:

This is a book.

Do you see that house over there?


This is used for pointing to a thing or a person which is close to the speaker.

Example 1: I need this bottle. (The bottle is very close to the speaker)

That is used for pointing to a thing or a person which is further away from the speaker.

Example 2: I need that bottle. (The bottle is further away from the speaker than in the first example)


These and Those

We use these and those if we want to point out multiple things (plural).

Examples:

These flowers are lovely.

I don't like those buildings.


This is used for pointing to things or people which are close to the speaker.

Example 1: These houses are very modern. (The speaker is standing right in front of these houses)

That is used for pointing to things or people which are further away from the speaker.

Example 2: Those houses are very modern. (The speaker is further away from the houses than in the first example)
