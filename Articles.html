Articles

Articles are words that identify a noun as being specific or unspecific. English has two types of articles: definite and indefinite.


Definite article

The definite article is the word the. The definite article can be used with singular and plural.

Examples:

Give me the hammer.

The beaches in Portugal are beautiful.

I didn't like the film.


IMPORTANT

We use the definite article to refer to something specific. For example: If your friend asks you: "Are you going to the party at the weekend?" Both you and your friend know which party he is talking about.


Indefinite article

The indefinite article takes two forms: a or an.

We use a when the next word begins with a consonant sound.

We use an when the next word begins with a vowel sound.

Examples:

I need a holiday.

Do you have an umbrella?

My neighbours have a dog.


IMPORTANT

The indefinite article can only be used with singular nouns.
