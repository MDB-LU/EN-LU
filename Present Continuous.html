Present Continuous

We use the Present Continuous to describe actions that are happening right now. In order to form the Present Continuous, we need the auxiliary verb be and the ing form of the main verb.

Examples:

He is going to the supermarket.

You are wearing a white shirt.

I am working in my garden.


Present Continuous - Negative

If we want to make a negative sentence in the Present Continuous, we need to add the word not. This word comes between the auxiliary verb and the main verb.

Examples:

I'm not watching television.

You are not listening.

He isn't cooking.


Present Continuous - Questions

If we want to ask a question in the Present Continuous, we have to put the verb be in front of the subject.

Examples:

Are you working in your garden?

Is he eating his food?

What am I doing here?
