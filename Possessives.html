Possessives

A possessive is a word that modifies a noun by identifying who has ownership or possession of it.

Examples:

This is my house.

His car was expensive.

Is this John's dog?


Possessive adjectives

Every person has its own possessive adjective:

I = my

You = your

He = his

She = her

It = its

We = our

You = your

They = Their


Possessive 's

We can use the possessive 's to talk about the relationship between people or to say who owns something. Possessive 's always comes after a noun or a name.

Examples:

This is Jack's computer.

Jane's hair is very long.

This car's windshield is broken.


IMPORTANT

Be careful: The possessive 's and the abbreviation for is is not the same.

Examples:

Tim's very intelligent. In this sentence, the 's stands for is.

Tim's brother is very intelligent. In this sentence, the 's is the possessive 's.
