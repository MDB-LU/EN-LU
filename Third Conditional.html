Third Conditional

You already know that there are four types of conditional sentences. This one is called the Third Conditional.

As every conditional sentence, this one also consists of two parts: The conditional clause and the main clause.


We can make a third conditional sentence with the past perfect after in the conditional clause and the words would have and the past participle in the main clause.

If + past perfect / would + have + past participle

The third conditional talks about the past. It is used to describe a situation that did not happen, and to imagine the result of this situation.

Examples:

If she had studied, she would have passed the exam.

I wouldn't have felt sick if I hadn't eaten so much.


Quick Check

Select the correct second conditional sentence:


1.

He would have became a teacher, if he had gone to university. He would have become a teacher if he had gone to university.

2.

If they went to bed earlier, they wouldn't have been tired. If they had gone to bed earlier, they wouldn't have been tired.

3.

If Luke wouldn't have sent flowers to his mother, she wasn't happy. If Luke hadn't sent flowers to his mother, she wouldn't have been happy.
