Future Perfect Continuous

We use the Future Perfect Continuous when we are looking back to the past from a point in the future and we want to emphasise the length or duration of an activity or event.

To make the future perfect continuous, we have to use the word will, the auxiliary verb have been and the -ing form of the main verb.

Examples:

Next August, the teacher will have been working at the school for 30 years.

In September, she will have been living in Australia for a year.

I will have been studying Spanish for three years by the end of this course.


Negative

If we want to make a negative sentence in the future perfect continuous, we have to add the word not after will and before have been.

We can also use the abbreviation won't which stands for will not.

Examples:

Next August, the teacher will not have been working at the same school for 30 years.

In September, she won't have been living in Australia for a year.

I will not have been studying Spanish for three years by the end of this course.


Questions

We can also make questions in the future perfect continuous. We just have to put will before the subject.

Examples:

Will she have been working here for three years next Monday?

Will they have been living together for a year tomorrow?


IMPORTANT

We can use the word shall instead of will for the person I and we.

Shall is more formal and less common than will.

Example:

Next week, I shall have been working here for ten years.
