Reported Speech

Reported speech is how we represent the speech of other people or ourselves.

There are two main types of reported speech: direct speech and indirect speech.


Direct Speech

Direct speech repeats the same words the person used:

Examples:

Kimberly said, “I realise it was a mistake.”

"I'm hungry", said James.

"She doesn't like you", said John.


Indirect Speech

In indirect speech, the original speaker’s words are changed.

Examples:

Kimberly said that she realised it was a mistake.

James said that he was hungry.

John told me that she didn't like me.


Backshift

When we change a sentence from direct speech to indirect speech, we often use a tense which is "further back" in the past. This is called a backshift.

Here is an examples:

Direct Speech: "I travel a lot", said Harry.

Indirect Speech: Harry said that he travelled a lot.

In the direct speech, we have the Present Simple (travel). In the indirect speech, this changes to the Past Simple (travelled).


Here's how the different tenses change in reported speech:

Present Simple:

The present simple changes to the past simple.

Example: "I travel a lot", said Harry. = Harry said that he travelled a lot.

Present Continuous:

The present continuous changes to the past continuous.

Example: "I'm travelling a lot", said Harry. = Harry said that he was travelling a lot.

Present Perfect:

The present perfect changes to the past perfect.

Example: "I have travelled a lot", said Harry. = Harry said that he had travelled a lot.

Past Simple:

The past simple changes to the past perfect.

Example: "I travelled a lot", said Harry. = Harry said that he had travelled a lot.

Past Continuous:

The past continuous changes to the past perfect continuous.

Example: "I was travelling a lot", said Harry. = Harry said that he had been travelling a lot.


Pronouns, demonstratives and adverbs

Pronouns usually change in indirect speech.

Example:

Direct Speech: "I enjoy working in my garden," said Bob.

Indirect Speech: Bob said that he enjoyed working in his garden.


Demonstratives and adverbs of time and place change as well, especially when they are no longer accurate.

Examples:

Direct Speech: "This is my car", he said.

Indirect Speech: He told me that was his car.

Direct Speech: "I like it here", she said.

Indirect Speech: She said that she liked it there.

Direct Speech: "I came home yesterday", I said.

Indirect Speech: I said that I had come home the day before.
