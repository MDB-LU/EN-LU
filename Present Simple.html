Present Simple

We use the Present Simple to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements.


Present Simple - Positive

If we want to make a positive sentence in the Present Simple, we can use the base form (infinitive) of the verb.

Examples:

I work in a bank.

They play football in a club.

We celebrate Christmas every year.


Present Simple - Negative

We can also make negative sentences in the Present Simple. For this, we have to use an auxiliary verb. Most of the time, this auxiliary verb is don't.

Don't stands for do not.

Examples:

I don't work in a bank.

They don't play football.

We don't celebrate Christmas.


Present Simple - Questions

If we want to ask a question in the Present Simple, we have to use an auxiliary verb. Most of the time, this auxiliary verb is do.

Examples:

Do you work in a bank?

Do they play football?

Do I look good?

IMPORTANT

We can use the base form (infinitive) of the verb in positive sentences, the auxiliary verb don't in negative sentences, and the auxiliary verb do in every person except for the third person singular.

Examples:

First person singular: I work in a bank. I don't work in a bank. Do I work in a bank?

Second person singular: You work in a bank. You don't work in a bank. Do you work in a bank?

First person plural: We work in a bank. We don't work in a bank. Do we work in a bank?.

Second person plural: You work in a bank. You don't work in a bank. Do you work in a bank?

Third person plural: work in a bank. They don't work in a bank. Do they work in a bank?.
