Defining and Non-Defining Relative Clauses - Practice

Complete the following exercises. At the end of the page, you will find a button called Check Answers. Click this button and you will find out your score and your mistakes.

Exercise 1

Read each sentence and identify whether the relative clause is defining or non-defining.

1: The shoes that I bought yesterday are too small.

2: My grandmother, who is 85 years old, loves to bake.

3: The phone which I lost last week has been found.

4: The river, which flows through the city, is very polluted.

5: The hotel that we stayed in was luxurious.

6: The painting, which was created in the 19th century, is very famous.

Exercise 2

Combine the following pairs of sentences using defining relative clauses.

7: I have a neighbor. He owns a bakery.

8: The dog is barking loudly. It belongs to our neighbor.

9: There is a library. People often go there to study.

10: The café has excellent coffee. It is located on Main Street.

Exercise 3

Combine the following pairs of sentences using non-defining relative clauses.

11: My cousin is visiting from Australia. She loves hiking.

12: London is a vibrant city. It has a rich history.

13: The painting sold for a million dollars. It was created by a renowned artist.

14: Sarah is going to Paris. She studied French in college.
