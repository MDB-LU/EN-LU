The Passive - Transformation

In English, we have the active and the passive voice. We can turn an active sentence into a passive one, and the other way around. On this page, you will learn how to do this.


Step-by-Step Transformation

Here you will find a step-by-step description of how to transform an active sentence into a passive one.


Step 1:

In order to transform an active sentence into a passive one, we need to identify the subject, verb and object of the sentence. Here is an example:

My father makes a cake.

My father is the subject.

makes is the verb.

a cake is the object.


Step 2:

The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.

That means, that A cake is now at the beginning of the sentence.


Step 3:

Next, we have to identify the tense of the verb in the active sentence.

makes is in the Present Simple, so we also need the Present Simple in the passive sentence.


Step 4:

Now we transform the verb into the passive. Remember, that we need the verb "be" and the past participle to make the passive. The verb "be" adopts the tense (Present Simple).

Passive verb form in the Present Simple: is made


Step 5:

The last step is to add the subject of the active sentence.

Here, it is very important that we us the preposition by before the word.

Example: by my father.


If we put everything together, we get the following passive sentence:

A cake is made by my father.


Quick Check

Choose the correct passive sentence.


1. The chef prepared a delicious meal.

The chef was prepared a delicious meal.
A delicious meal was prepared by the chef.
A delicious meal is prepared the chef.

2. The manager approves the budget

The budget is approved by the manager.
The budget was approved by the manager.
The budget approved the manager.

3. The artist is painting a portrait.

The artist is being painted by a portrait.
A portrait is painted by the artist.
A portrait is being painted by the artist.
