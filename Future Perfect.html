Future Perfect

We use the Future Perfect when we look back to the past from a point in the future.

To make the future perfect, we have to use the word will, the auxiliary verb have and the past participle of the main verb.

Examples:

Next August, I will have worked ten years for this company.

I will have finished the essay by next Monday.

They will have stayed in Italy for three months tomorrow.


Negative

If we want to make a negative sentence in the future perfect, we have to add the word not after will and before have.

We can also use the abbreviation won't which stands for will not.

Examples:

They will not have finished their project by Friday.

He won't have eaten breakfast before nine.

We will not go on holiday before the end of the year.


Questions

We can also make questions in the future perfect. We just have to put will before the subject.

Examples:

Will you have eaten lunch already when we arrive?

Will they have changed their outfit before the break?


IMPORTANT

The future perfect is only for actions that will be complete before a point in the future.

The action you’re talking about must have a deadline. This deadline can be very specific (eight o’clock) or it can be vague (next week), it just has to be some time in the future.
